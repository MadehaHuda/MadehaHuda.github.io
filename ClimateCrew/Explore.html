Jamuna

River erosion

Erosion occurs when river water eat away the land's surface over time. Bangladesh is in danger because it's located on a large river delta.

Due to climate change, there are more floods and heavy rains which is increasing river erosion. This is destroying people’s homes and farmlands.

Bangladesh has 3 mighty rivers, Padma, Meghna and Jamuna, and they have taken away a lot of land – 5 times the size of the capital city!

Dhaka

Temperature rise

Over the years, Bangladesh has become warmer with temperatures increasing by 0.5°C on average between 1976 and 2019.

When the weather gets hotter and more humid, people are more likely to get sick with respiratory infections. Diseases carried by mosquitoes also increase.

In Dhaka, some places are much hotter than others. Poor areas are hotter because they lack trees and the houses are made of metal sheets that trap heat.

Buriganga

Pollution

The Buriganga river is an important part of Dhaka’s economy and heritage. It is a source of drinking water, it helps with trade, and it is used for transportation everyday.

Sadly, the river is now very polluted with things like chemicals from factories, sewage, and garbage, which is not good for the people or the animals that live there.

During the British era, the Buriganga area was famous for its scenic beauty!

Bandarban

Landslide

A landslide is when the soil and rocks on a hill start to move down the hill really fast. This is very dangerous for the people living there.

In Chattogram hill tracts, people are cutting down the hills and making them unstable.

So when it rains a lot, the soil on the hills gets really loose and causes landslides to occur.

Sundarban

Deforestation

Deforestation is the cutting down of trees in large numbers. As populations increase, people need more resources like wood.

Mangrove forests like Sundarbans are threatened by deforestation because they need very specific conditions to survive. They only grow in certain areas.

Many people depend on the Sundarbans for their livelihoods. Timber and prawn farming are the major reasons for deforestation here.

Sundarban Bay

Salinization

Salinization is when the salt water from the sea come further inland and make the soil salty. In the 1970s, the fresh water coming to the Sundarbans from the Ganges river decreased.

This has caused many problems for people who live in the area, including making it hard to grow crops and catch fish.

It's also hurting the mangrove trees and animals that live there. People find it difficult to have enough food and live in a healthy environment.

Cox's Bazar

Cyclone

A cyclone is a big, swirling storm that happens over warm ocean water. When it gets too close to land, it can cause a lot of damage

The cyclones usually happen before monsoon, from April to May, or in the late rainy season, from October to November.

In fact, over the past 200 years, there have been 60 severe cyclones that have hit Bangladesh!

Sylhet

Flash flood

A flash flood is a sudden, rapid flooding that can happen after a heavy rain. Water can rise very quickly and cause a lot of damage.

In June 2022, Sylhet experienced a severe flash flood, which was caused by record rainfall levels in nearby Indian states.

It affected millions of people, many of them lost their homes. Many areas lost access to electricity and medical service.
